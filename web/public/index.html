About

kbImageScramble turns a photograph into a 15-puzzle with hundreds of thousands of tiles — every single pixel is a tile, with one hole. Load any image and scramble it: the hole wanders the picture swapping pixels as it goes, and somewhere past a few million moves the photo dissolves into static. Then hit Solve!! and watch it come back — the solver routes the hole around the board to walk each pixel home, filling in the image row by row like it's being redrawn, and it works no matter how scrambled the board is (a billion moves? doesn't matter). Flip!! solves the puzzle into the upside-down image instead; Swirl scrambles with a drifting bias so the photo smears instead of dissolving; and Stuipd Solve [sic] tries to solve greedily and visibly fails forever — a demonstration of why the real solver has to be clever.

It began as a brother-to-brother coding challenge in January 2008. The engine running here is that original C++ code, compiled unmodified to WebAssembly; the 2008 sources are preserved offline and may be published someday. Code for this app is on GitHub.
